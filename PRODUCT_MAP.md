# Product Map

This is the build queue after the archive is organized. It prevents new
projects from being started before the existing recipes are turned into usable
systems.

## Two-Week Alignment Gate

This phase is complete when:

- The GitHub first screen is understandable in 30 seconds.
- The public/private boundary is fixed in `PUBLIC_PRIVATE_SPLIT.md`.
- Every project can be explained as one judgment-layer lineage.
- Cognitive OS is visible as the flagship face.
- Financial Agent Evidence OS is visible as evidence discipline, not a trading
  product.
- X-MoD and Angelos OS are visible as the autonomous-driving research roots.
- The next implementation step is visible from this product map.

This phase does not include starting new projects, adding new product features,
rewriting all paper drafts, publishing sensitive private material, or making
unbounded benchmark, AGI, safety, performance, or guarantee claims.

## Priority 1: Cognitive OS Demo Polish

**Purpose:** make the LLM decision protocol understandable to a mentor or
researcher in under three minutes.

**User:** someone reviewing AI governance, model safety, or human-in-the-loop
decision systems.

**MVP:**

- One demo flow with a user profile, candidate output, gate decision, and public
  decision envelope.
- Evidence summary page that explains gate accuracy, trace completeness, and
  conformance in plain language.
- Clear trace privacy behavior: public redaction by default, raw trace only by
  explicit local opt-in.

**Later version:**

- More real-provider tests.
- Adversarial scenario pack.
- Exportable evidence report.

## Priority 2: Financial Agent Evidence OS Dashboard

**Purpose:** show evidence discipline without making the project look like a
trading product.

**User:** reviewer evaluating whether an AI-agent claim is scoped and
reproducible.

**MVP:**

- Read-only evidence viewer.
- Claim / non-claim panel.
- Reproduction command panel.
- Evidence manifest and release-seal viewer.

**Later version:**

- Side-by-side release comparison.
- Tamper detection walkthrough.
- Reviewer checklist export.

## Priority 3: X-MoD Visual Paper Kit

**Purpose:** make the autonomous driving explainability work legible without
requiring the reader to parse every report.

**User:** research mentor, workshop reviewer, or collaborator.

**Current public status:** v1 kit available at
[research/xmod-visual-paper-kit](research/xmod-visual-paper-kit/README.md).

**MVP:**

- Architecture diagram for four-drive routing.
- Gate-event alignment plot.
- Negative-control summary.
- One-page limitation and non-claim sheet.

**Done when:**

- A reader can understand the four-drive architecture from one diagram.
- The safety-gate alignment result is visible as a plot, not only a table.
- Negative controls are explained as part of the method, not as an appendix.
- The kit states that this is research evidence, not deployment readiness.

**Later version:**

- Related work table.
- Additional drive-alignment checks.
- External-baseline comparison plan.

## Priority 4: Angelos / CARLA Reproducibility Kit

**Purpose:** separate the judgment-layer idea from overbroad safety claims.

**User:** autonomous driving or robotics reviewer.

**Current public status:** v1 kit available at
[research/angelos-reproducibility-kit](research/angelos-reproducibility-kit/README.md).

**MVP:**

- Protocol description.
- Scenario list.
- Result table.
- Failure-analysis section.
- Reproduction requirements.

**Done when:**

- The protocol can be read without opening every paper draft.
- Each result table names scenario, seed count, metric, and claim boundary.
- Mobility-freeze and legality tradeoffs are visible in the main evidence page.
- The kit avoids universal safety language and separates local CARLA evidence
  from real-world deployment claims.

**Later version:**

- Containerized or scripted run path.
- Scenario screenshots.
- Trace viewer.

## Guardrails

- Do not start a new project until the current priority has a clear demo and
  evidence page.
- Do not add broad claims without a claim boundary.
- Do not use financial or safety language as promotion.
- Do not publish private admissions material or personal documents.
